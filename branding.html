Personal Branding

Exploring my identity as a designer

How do designers design for themselves? That's the question I was asked when I first started this project for University. The answer was slightly more involved that I initially thought. At first we were tasked with designing a logo, wordmark and visual marque along with a set of brand guidelines for ourselves, but I decided to take it a little bit further with colour variations and visual extensions, like wallpapers and patterns.

The problems

The biggest, most glaring problem was that personal branding was something that I, as a designer, was in dire need of. I'll admit, I was dreading this project, I'd experimented with monograms in the past and found matching the g letterform to literally anything a struggle, nevermind something as linear as an I. Additionally, despite my previous attempts, I'd never made something that I would have been confident showing off. It was time to knuckle down and make cool stuff.
